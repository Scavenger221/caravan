How to Play

22 25 18
two of your three caravans sold

The Goal

Build three caravans (card piles) worth 21–26 — that's a sold caravan. Each caravan competes with the opponent's caravan in the same lane; the higher sold caravan wins the lane. Win 2 of 3 lanes to take the pot.

4♠→1 9♦→2 6♣→3
one number card on each empty caravan

Setup

You start with 8 cards. Your first three turns must each place one number card (A–10) on an empty caravan of yours. After that you draw back up to 5 cards each turn.

3♠7♠ 9♣ 5♠ 4♦
3♠ → 7♠ is ascending: 9♣ continues up, 5♠ matches suit, 4♦ is illegal

Number Cards

Played only on your own caravans (Ace = 1). Two cards set a direction. The next card must continue the direction or match the top card's suit — and never repeat the same value.

8♥✕J 8♥K=16
Jack removes · King doubles

Jack & King

Face cards attach to any placed card — yours or the opponent's. A Jack removes the card it's played on. A King doubles that card's value, and Kings stack (8 → 16 → 32).

▲Q♥→▼ A♠★→5♠✕9♠✕
Queen flips direction · Joker wipes matching cards

Queen & Joker

A Queen goes on a caravan's top card: it reverses the direction and changes the suit to hers. A Joker on an Ace removes every other card of that suit on the table; on a 2–10 it removes every other card of that value.

Caps & Extras

Every match has a bet; the winner takes the pot. Instead of playing you may discard a card, or disband one of your caravans (tap its value badge). Over 26 is overburdened — fix it with a Jack or disband. Go broke and the house stakes you 50 caps.
